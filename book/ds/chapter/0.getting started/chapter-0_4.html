Chapter 0.4

Types of Data Problems

10 min read Beginner Foundation

Not all data problems are created equal. Understanding the type of problem you're facing determines which tools and techniques to use.

The Relationship

These three terms are often used interchangeably, but they represent different concepts with significant overlap. Think of them as nested circles in a Venn diagram.

Simple Rule: AI is the broadest concept, ML is a subset of AI, and Data Science uses both ML and AI as tools while adding statistical analysis and domain expertise.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI is the broadest term, referring to any system that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. This includes reasoning, learning, perception, language understanding, and problem-solving.

What is AI?

Systems that can perceive their environment, reason about it, and take actions to achieve specific goals.

Types of AI

Includes rule-based systems, expert systems, neural networks, and everything in between.

Examples

Chess engines, virtual assistants, recommendation systems, autonomous vehicles.

Machine Learning (ML)

Machine Learning is a subset of AI focused on systems that learn from data. Instead of being explicitly programmed with rules, ML algorithms identify patterns and make decisions based on examples.

1

Supervised Learning

Learning from labeled examples (e.g., spam detection, house price prediction).

2

Unsupervised Learning

Finding patterns in unlabeled data (e.g., customer segmentation, anomaly detection).

3

Reinforcement Learning

Learning through trial and error with rewards (e.g., game AI, robotics).

Data Science

Data Science is an interdisciplinary field that uses scientific methods, processes, and systems to extract knowledge from structured and unstructured data. It encompasses statistics, ML, domain expertise, and communication.

"A data scientist is someone who is better at statistics than any software engineer and better at software engineering than any statistician."

— Josh Wills

Key Differences

AI Focus

  • Building intelligent systems
  • Mimicking human cognition
  • Automation of complex tasks
  • May or may not use data-driven approaches

ML Focus

  • Pattern recognition from data
  • Prediction and classification
  • Improving with experience
  • Always data-driven

Data Science Focus

  • Extracting insights from data
  • Answering business questions
  • Statistical analysis and experimentation
  • Communication and storytelling
  • Uses ML as one of many tools

A Practical Example

Consider a company wanting to reduce customer churn:

AI

The Vision

Create an intelligent system that automatically identifies at-risk customers and takes action to retain them.

ML

The Engine

Build a model that predicts which customers are likely to churn based on their behavior patterns.

DS

The Process

Analyze customer data, understand why they leave, test retention strategies, measure impact, and communicate findings to stakeholders.

Bottom Line: AI is the goal, ML is the technique, and Data Science is the comprehensive approach that includes both plus statistics, domain knowledge, and communication.
