Chapter 0.4

Types of Data Problems

10 min read Beginner Foundation

Not all data problems are created equal. Understanding the type of problem you're facing determines which tools and techniques to use.

Four Main Categories

Data science problems generally fall into four categories, often called the "analytics maturity model." Each builds on the previous one, increasing in complexity and business value.

Key Insight: Most organizations start with descriptive analytics ("What happened?") and gradually progress toward prescriptive analytics ("What should we do?").
1

Descriptive Analytics

What happened? Understanding historical data and current state.

2

Diagnostic Analytics

Why did it happen? Finding root causes and relationships.

3

Predictive Analytics

What will happen? Forecasting future outcomes.

4

Prescriptive Analytics

What should we do? Recommending optimal actions.

Descriptive Analytics: What Happened?

Descriptive analytics summarizes raw data into something understandable. It answers "what happened" by looking at historical data and creating reports, dashboards, and visualizations.

Common Techniques

Aggregations, averages, counts, percentages, data visualization, dashboards.

Questions Answered

How many sales did we make? What was our revenue? Who are our top customers?

Business Examples

Monthly sales reports, website traffic analytics, customer demographics analysis.

"Without descriptive analytics, you're flying blind. It's the foundation that everything else builds on."

— Data Analytics Principle

Diagnostic Analytics: Why Did It Happen?

Diagnostic analytics goes deeper to understand the causes behind what happened. It identifies patterns, correlations, and relationships in the data.

Common Techniques

  • Correlation analysis
  • Drill-down analysis
  • Root cause analysis
  • Cohort analysis
  • A/B testing

Business Examples

  • Why did sales drop last quarter?
  • Which marketing channels drive conversions?
  • What factors lead to customer churn?
  • Why do certain products sell together?
Important: Correlation doesn't equal causation. Diagnostic analytics can identify relationships, but establishing true causality often requires controlled experiments.

Predictive Analytics: What Will Happen?

Predictive analytics uses historical data and statistical models to forecast future outcomes. This is where machine learning often comes into play.

Common Techniques

Regression models, classification algorithms, time series forecasting, neural networks.

Questions Answered

How many units will we sell? Which customers will churn? What's the risk of default?

Business Examples

Customer lifetime value prediction, demand forecasting, credit risk scoring.

Prescriptive Analytics: What Should We Do?

Prescriptive analytics is the most advanced type, recommending specific actions to achieve desired outcomes. It combines predictions with optimization and decision science.

Common Techniques

  • Optimization algorithms
  • Simulation modeling
  • Decision trees
  • Reinforcement learning
  • Recommendation systems

Business Examples

  • Dynamic pricing strategies
  • Inventory optimization
  • Personalized product recommendations
  • Route optimization for delivery
  • Resource allocation

Choosing the Right Approach

The type of problem you're solving should drive your analytical approach. Here's how to decide:

1

Assess Your Question

Does it ask what, why, what will, or what should? This points to the analytics type.

2

Check Data Availability

Predictive and prescriptive analytics require substantial historical data.

3

Consider Business Value

Sometimes a simple descriptive answer is enough—don't overcomplicate.

4

Build Progressively

Master descriptive before diagnostic, diagnostic before predictive.

Practical Tip: Many organizations jump straight to machine learning without solid descriptive and diagnostic foundations. Start simple, prove value, then advance.

Real-World Problem Classification

Let's classify some common business scenarios:

Retail Example

Descriptive: Weekly sales summary
Diagnostic: Why sales dropped in region X
Predictive: Next month's demand forecast
Prescriptive: Optimal inventory levels per store

Healthcare Example

Descriptive: Patient admission trends
Diagnostic: Factors in readmission rates
Predictive: Disease risk prediction
Prescriptive: Personalized treatment plans

Marketing Example

Descriptive: Campaign performance report
Diagnostic: Which channels drive ROI
Predictive: Customer lifetime value
Prescriptive: Optimal budget allocation

"The goal isn't to do the most advanced analytics—it's to solve the right problem with the appropriate level of sophistication."

— Data Science Best Practice
