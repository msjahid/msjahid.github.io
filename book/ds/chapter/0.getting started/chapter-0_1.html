What Is Data Science?

8 min read Beginner Foundation

Data Science is the art and science of extracting meaningful insights from data. It combines statistics, programming, domain expertise, and storytelling to solve real-world problems.

The Core Definition

At its heart, data science is about asking the right questions and finding answers hidden in data. Unlike traditional statistics, which focuses on mathematical rigor, data science emphasizes practical problem-solving and actionable insights.

Key Insight: Data science is not just about algorithms—it's about understanding business problems, cleaning messy data, and communicating findings effectively.

Three Pillars of Data Science

Statistics & Math

The foundation for understanding patterns, relationships, and uncertainty in data.

Programming & Tools

Python, R, SQL, and frameworks that turn theory into working solutions.

Domain Expertise

Understanding the business context and knowing which questions matter.

The Data Science Workflow

Every data science project follows a similar pattern, though the specifics vary by domain and problem type.

1

Define the Problem

What question are we trying to answer? What does success look like?

2

Collect & Clean Data

Gather relevant data and handle missing values, outliers, and errors.

3

Explore & Visualize

Understand patterns, distributions, and relationships in the data.

4

Model & Analyze

Apply statistical methods or machine learning algorithms.

5

Communicate Results

Present findings in a clear, actionable way to stakeholders.

"Data science is 90% cleaning data and 10% complaining about cleaning data."

— Popular Data Science Saying

Essential Skills

To succeed in data science, you'll need a balanced mix of technical and soft skills:

Technical Skills

  • Python or R programming
  • SQL for data manipulation
  • Statistics and probability
  • Machine learning fundamentals
  • Data visualization

Soft Skills

  • Critical thinking
  • Communication
  • Business acumen
  • Curiosity and creativity
  • Ethical judgment
